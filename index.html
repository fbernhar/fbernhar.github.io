KiCad workshop
===============

A friendly approach to KiCad, circuit simulation, and PCB design, using the classic Fuzz Face pedal as an example project.

We will draw the schematic, simulate the circuit, explore how the fuzz effect works, create a PCB layout, and export the final files for manufacturing.

Repository
----------

Workshop files, KiCad project, documentation, and material can be found here:

https://github.com/fbernhar/pub_fuzzface/tree/main/src/kicad

For whom is this workshop?
--------------------------

This workshop is for:

  • people who never used KiCad before
  • people interested in electronics or guitar pedals
  • anyone who wants to build a simple analog distortion circuit

The content is fully self-contained and available online. Meaning accessible for everyone and for free :)

Requirements
------------
  • basic computer skills
  • laptop with KiCad installed
  • optional: git if you want to clone the repository
  • optional later: soldering iron, solder, side cutters, and a multimeter
Material
--------

We use a simple Fuzz Face style circuit with:

  • 2 x AC128 or similar PNP germanium transistors
  • 4 resistors: 33k, 100k, 470 Ohm, 8.2k
  • 3 capacitors: 2.2uF, 20uF or 22uF, 0.01uF
  • 1 x 1k (Lin) potentiometer for fuzz
  • 1 x 500k (Log) potentiometer for volume
  • 1 x 9V battery
  • 2 x audio jacks
  • wire connections for off-board parts
Estimated expenses
------------------

Very roughly:

  • basic components: about 15 - 20 EUR
  • AC128 transistors: about 5 - 10 EUR
  • PCB manufacturing: about 5 - 10 EUR + shipping
  • optional: enclosure, footswitch, knobs, etc.: extra expenses

So for a simple workshop build, the total is often somewhere around:

20 - 40 EUR.

Workshop content
----------------

Part I: schematic editor

  • create a project
  • place components
  • wire the circuit

Part II: simulation

  • DC operating point analysis
  • transient analysis
  • FFT / frequency content analysis

Part III: PCB editor

  • assign footprints
  • place components
  • route tracks

Part IV: fabrication output

  • export Gerber files
  • export drill files
  • check the output
What we will learn
-------------------
  • how to create a schematic in KiCad
  • how to simulate a circuit with SPICE
  • how to assign footprints and design a simple PCB
  • how to prepare manufacturing files
How we want to learn together
---------------
I would love this to be a friendly and welcoming environment where everyone feels free to ask questions, try things out, and make mistakes without fear of judgment. No one is expected to already know anything. Questions are welcome at any point, and there is no such thing as a "stupid" question. Let us look out for each other, check in with each other, and offer help when it seems wanted or needed. Please be mindful of how much space you take up, especially if you are already more experienced or used to technical spaces and technical language. Sharing knowledge is great, but it should support people rather than make them feel small.

P.S. This is my first workshop, so I am learning alongside you in some ways. I will try my best to guide us through this, but I also appreciate your patience if something takes a moment, if I am puzzled myself, or if we need to figure things out together as a group :)

Disclaimer
----------

This workshop is a learning project. Before ordering or soldering a real PCB, always double-check footprints, transistor pinout, capacitor polarity, track routing, ground connections, and the exported Gerber files.
